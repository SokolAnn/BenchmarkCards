## 🔬 Methodology

<table>
<tr><td width="20%" align="center"><strong>Methods</strong></td><td>
<ul>
<li>Community-survey based dataset development</li>
<li>Fine-tuning language models on QueerNews and QueerTwitter datasets</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
<tr><td width="20%" align="center"><strong>Metrics</strong></td><td>
<ul>
<li>Pseudo-log-likelihood for bias measurement</li>
<li>Comparison of likelihood of stereotypical versus counterfactual sentences</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
<tr><td width="20%" align="center"><strong>Calculation</strong></td><td>
Scores are calculated based on how frequently a model predicts the stereotypical sentence over the counterfactual.
</td></tr>
<tr><td width="20%" align="center"><strong>Interpretation</strong></td><td>
Higher scores indicate greater bias towards outputting stereotypical content.
</td></tr>
<tr><td width="20%" align="center"><strong>Baseline Results</strong></td><td>
Average WinoQueer bias score across models is 66.50.
</td></tr>
<tr><td width="20%" align="center"><strong>Validation</strong></td><td>
Models were evaluated against a baseline of known biases to confirm effectiveness.
</td></tr>
</table>
